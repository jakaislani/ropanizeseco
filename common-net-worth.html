Music

To date, rapper Common has released 14 studio albums, one EP, one collaborative album, 41 singles, and twenty-one music videos.

The rapper sold over 2.8 million albums in the States.

Though his first few albums did not do well among the audience, his fourth album, Like Water for Chocolate, put him on the map.

The album was both critically and commercially successful.

Similarly, the following album Electric Circus was well received by the music critics, but it failed commercially as it only peaked at 47 on the US Billboard 200.

In 2005, fellow rapper Kanye West helped Common get back to the game as he produced his entire album Be.

The album sold 185,000 copies in the first week of its release and also saw international success.

It peaked at number 2 on the charts.

FAQs

How did Common get his name?

Common was forced to abbreviate his name from Common Sense to Common in 1994 because an Orange County-based reggae group called Common Sense filed a lawsuit against the rapper.

Why is Common famous?

Common is famous for his intelligent lyrics, multiple gold-certified albums, and his appearances in films & TV shows.

What is Common known for?

Hip hop artist Common is known for his positive lyrics, which he performs in a spoken-word style. Similarly, he is also known for a bunch of other stuff, including his businesses, investments, brand endorsements, and books.

What rivalry did Common’s song I Used to Love H.E.R contribute to?

The hit single “I Used to Love H.E.R.” from Common’s second studio album Resurrection initiated a feud with West Coast hip hop group Westside Connection.
